About Me

Hello! I am Jinze Li (李金泽), an undergraduate student in the Economics (Top Talent Program) at the School of Business, Nanjing University. My research interests span monetary policy, industrial economics, spatial economics, and applied econometrics, with a focus on causal inference, DSGE modeling, and interdisciplinary research combining economics with computer vision and spatial data science.

I have led and participated in multiple high-level academic research projects, including the optimization of China's characteristic interest rate corridor, distributed solar energy intelligent detection in South Africa, and the capacity governance effect of mandatory exit policies for zombie firms. My academic work has been widely recognized, with awards such as the Second Prize of the 36th "Silver Star Cup" Undergraduate Academic Paper Competition, and the Excellence Award of Nanjing University Basic Discipline Scholarship.

Beyond research, I am deeply committed to academic community building and student leadership. I serve as Deputy Director of the Overseas Exchange Center of the Youth Liaison Department of the CYLC Nanjing University Committee, Secretary of the Youth League Branch of the 2024 Economics Top Talent Class, and a founding member of the Nanjing University Economics Association.

Research Projects

Optimization Path of China's Characteristic Interest Rate Corridor: An Empirical Study Based on Improving Monetary Policy Transmission Efficiency: Led the project, constructed a DSGE model, and verified that interest-bearing wholesale CBDC can enhance the sensitivity and smoothness of monetary policy transmission by hardening the lower bound of the interest rate corridor.
Distributed Solar in South Africa: As the only undergraduate in the joint team of Cambridge University and LSE, built an end-to-end automated solution for intelligent detection and spatial interpretation of large-scale rooftop PV panels using deep learning, computer vision, Google Earth, QGIS and Codex, with related results open-sourced on GitHub.
Camphor Economics Chinese Library: As a tweet author, shared cutting-edge academic papers on the platform, with a single tweet receiving nearly 1,000 reads and nearly 100 forwards.
"Nanzhi Liaoxing" Social Practice Research Group: Founded a cross-grade, cross-major research team with 11 members, advised by Prof. Qiang Wu. The team was awarded the university-level excellent project, and conducted in-depth research on equipment manufacturing enterprises, development zones and research institutes.
